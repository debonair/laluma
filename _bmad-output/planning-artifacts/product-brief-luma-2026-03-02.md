# Product Brief: La Luma

## Executive Summary

La Luma is the world's first purpose-built digital ecosystem exclusively for single mothers — combining community, identity, and utility into one cohesive platform. While platforms like Peanut address general motherhood, no dedicated global product exists for single mothers: a demographic with distinct emotional, financial, and social realities. La Luma closes that gap by providing a safe, values-driven digital home paired with real-world connection, curated events (Luma Spaces), and authentic brand partnerships — delivering measurable social impact alongside compelling commercial opportunity.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Single mothers represent one of the most underserved demographics in consumer technology. Despite accounting for millions of households globally, they are treated as a niche subset of general motherhood rather than a primary audience deserving dedicated infrastructure. Existing platforms — including Peanut, Facebook Groups, and local community apps — serve the broad motherhood spectrum without acknowledging the unique emotional, financial, and social pressures specific to single motherhood. The lived reality of solo parenting demands purpose-built tools, not retrofitted features.

### Problem Impact

When single mothers lack a dedicated community:
- **Isolation compounds.** Without peers who share their specific reality, emotional support networks are thin or absent.
- **Practical needs go unmet.** Financial planning, childcare navigation, and career reentry require context-specific guidance not available in general parenting communities.
- **Brand access remains generic.** Single mothers have distinct purchasing behaviours and needs, yet receive no targeted value from brands who want to reach them.
- **Local connection is fragmented.** Location-based real-world belonging — critical for solo parents — is left to chance rather than facilitated by design.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Platform | Gap |
|---|---|
| **Peanut** | Broad motherhood audience; profile matching and chat only; no event infrastructure; no niche-focused brand partnerships |
| **Facebook Groups** | General-purpose, fragmented, not identity-safe, no integrated utility |
| **Local community apps** | Hyper-local only; no global identity layer; no scalable model |
| **Parenting forums** | Information-focused; no belonging, no real-world integration, no commercial ecosystem |

None of these treat single motherhood as a first-class identity. La Luma does.

### Proposed Solution

La Luma is a focused digital ecosystem built around five pillars:

1. **Safe Community** — A single-mother-only verified space for authentic connection, peer support, and shared experience
2. **Location-Based Connection** — Discovery and matching tools that facilitate real-world relationships and local support networks
3. **Luma Spaces** — Curated in-person and hybrid events where single mothers gather, learn, and build lasting networks; the platform's signature experiential layer
4. **Brand Integration** — A contextual, non-intrusive channel for aligned brands to offer discounts, services, content, and experiences specifically meaningful to single mothers
5. **Global + Local Model** — Scalable global infrastructure with localisation built in, maintaining unified brand identity while serving culturally diverse communities

### Key Differentiators

- **Identity ownership:** The only platform that treats single motherhood as the primary identity, not a segment tag
- **Utility-first community:** Moves beyond social networking into practical infrastructure (events, brand value, local belonging)
- **Commercial alignment:** Deep niche creates higher engagement, loyalty, and brand partnership ROI compared to broad motherhood platforms
- **Timing advantage:** Peanut has proven the market; no one has moved to capture the single mother segment at scale
- **Community + commerce balance:** Authentic community trust creates a premium, non-intrusive channel brands actively want access to

## Target Users

### Primary Users

#### The La Luma Woman — Core Persona

**"The woman who holds everything together — and rarely feels fully seen."**

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age Range** | 28–48 |
| **Status** | Recently separated, long-term divorced, never-partnered, or widowed |
| **Location** | Urban or suburban |
| **Life Stage** | Working full-time, running a business, studying, or managing household independently |
| **Parenting Stage** | Toddlers through teenagers; multiple-age children; or empty-nest transition |

**Her support reality:**
She may have family nearby. She may have friends. She may even have practical help. But she feels misunderstood in her lived experience, quietly judged for her circumstances, expected to "be strong" without space to process, and unable to express her reality without explanation.

**Her daily load:**
She carries the full mental load of decision-making. She manages multiple schedules, emotional needs, and financial pressures simultaneously. She is competent, capable — and tired.

La Luma is not for women who have failed to cope. It is for women who cope brilliantly — and need a space where that effort is understood without being explained.

---

### User Segments

#### A — Newly Single Mothers *(0–12 months)*
Emotionally raw and adjusting. Seeking validation, reassurance, and safe discussion. Highest need for community conversation and peer support. Discovery engine: word-of-mouth and crisis-search moments.

#### B — Established Solo Parents *(2+ years)*
More emotionally steady. Focused on growth, financial empowerment, and opportunity. Interested in quality networking, curated resources, and Luma Spaces events.

#### C — Multi-Aged Children Mothers
Managing simultaneous developmental needs across vastly different age groups. High mental load, time scarcity, complex scheduling. Needs efficiency, relatability, and peer understanding — not generic parenting advice.

#### D — Empty-Nest Mothers
Experiencing identity shifts after active parenting ends. Navigating loneliness, purpose redefinition, or second-life reinvention. Overlooked in traditional "mom" communities — La Luma is one of few spaces that acknowledges their continued identity as a single mother.

#### E — Age-Spectrum Considerations

| Age Band | Primary Need |
|---|---|
| 18–25 | Financial guidance, mentorship, practical safety net |
| 30s–40s | Efficiency, quality support, career-compatible community |
| Late 40s+ | Independence, legacy, identity beyond active parenting |

**Across all segments, one emotional truth holds:**
*Even when surrounded by people, she may not feel deeply understood.*

---

### Secondary Users

**Brand Partners**
Brands engage with La Luma as a curated, ethical channel to a high-loyalty, values-driven consumer segment. They offer relevant discounts, sponsor events, and deliver tailored content — not generic advertising. Participation is conditional on alignment with La Luma's community values.

**Admin & Editorial Team**
The platform team serves as community moderators, event curators, and cultural guardians. They protect psychological safety, maintain editorial standards, and ensure the tone, quality, and integrity of the La Luma experience remain consistent globally.

---

### User Journey

**Discovery**
She finds La Luma through a moment of need — a search, a recommendation from a peer, or a social media reference during a difficult period.

**Onboarding**
She creates a verified profile. The experience immediately signals difference: she is not one category in a broad parenting app. This space is built for her specific reality.

**Core Usage**
She joins community groups aligned to her life stage. She reads posts, contributes to conversations, discovers Luma Spaces events nearby, and receives content and offers tailored to her actual needs.

**The Aha! Moment**
She reads a post that mirrors her internal thoughts exactly. Or she shares something vulnerable and receives grounded, respectful support. Or she attends an event and realises she does not need to justify herself here.

Her internal response:
> *"I don't have to explain myself here."*
> *"I'm not the only one."*
> *"This space understands my reality."*

**Long-Term**
La Luma becomes a consistent environment in her life — not a crisis resource, but a home. Her strength is assumed. Her complexity is respected. Her experience is normalised.

## Success Metrics

### User Success

**What she does differently after joining La Luma:**
- Stops navigating challenges alone
- Shares experiences without over-explaining or defending her choices
- Engages with curated resources or events relevant to single mothers
- Builds meaningful connections, both online and offline

**Behavioural signals that she has found her people:**
- Returns to the platform 2–3 times per week
- Reads or interacts with 3–5 posts within her first week
- Posts, comments, or reacts at least once within the first week
- Joins one relevant sub-group or conversation
- Attends or registers for a Luma Spaces event within the first month

**Speed of belonging:**
Within the first 5–7 days, she should feel *"I belong here"* through visible, relatable conversations and relevant onboarding. Early wins are small but meaningful: one post that resonates, one thoughtful response received, one resource that applies to her actual life.

### Business Objectives

| Milestone | 3 Months Post-Launch | 12 Months Post-Launch |
|---|---|---|
| **Registered Users** | 500–1,000 | 5,000–10,000 |
| **Active Users** | 30–40% of registrants (weekly) | 35–45% monthly |
| **Luma Spaces Events** | 1–2 events, 50–100 participants each | 4–6 recurring events across multiple cities |
| **Brand Partnerships** | 1–2 aligned sponsors | 5–10 recurring partners |
| **Word-of-Mouth Referrals** | 10–15% of new users | Growing organically |

**Focus priority:** The 3-month focus is engagement and retention — not raw acquisition. The objective is to demonstrate that the product resonates with the right audience, not that it spreads quickly.

---

### Revenue Model

**Primary revenue:** Brand partnerships and sponsorships aligned with single mothers' lived needs.

**Secondary revenue:**
- Luma Spaces event ticketing (small-scale, local or virtual)
- Affiliate programmes for trusted, curated products and services

**Philosophy:** Community-first. Monetisation is layered slowly to protect trust.
**Early target:** 1–2 paying brand partners within 3–6 months, growing to 5–10 by end of year one.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

All features below exist in the current platform and are included in the La Luma MVP. Each requires deliberate reframing, copy revision, and in some cases UX rethinking to serve single mothers authentically rather than a general parenting audience.

| Feature | La Luma Reframing Required |
|---|---|
| **Authentication & Identity** | Onboarding reframed around single-mother life stage (newly single, established solo parent, empty-nester, etc.); verification strategy for community safety |
| **Community Groups** | Organised by life stage, location, and theme; anonymous posting retained as a trust-building feature; community guidelines written for this audience |
| **Posts, Comments & Polls** | Copy and prompts reframed for single-mother context; polls used for community voice |
| **Activity Feed** | Surfaces most relatable content first; onboarding guides new members to the right groups immediately |
| **Content — My Luma** | Editorial strategy rewritten for single mothers; sponsored content policy aligned with brand partnership values |
| **Content Submissions** | Becomes a channel for single mothers to contribute their own stories and expertise |
| **Social Connections** | Reframed for peer support and local connection; language shifted away from generic "connect" framing |
| **Direct Messaging** | Core peer-support utility; safety and moderation policies around DMs require explicit definition |
| **Notifications** | Copy and tone aligned with La Luma's voice |
| **Local Directory** | Priority categories: childcare, legal support, financial services, mental health, community spaces |
| **Subscriptions** | Tier names, benefits, and positioning rewritten for La Luma's values |
| **Admin & Moderation** | Elevated to cultural guardianship role; safety policies written specifically for psychological safety of single mothers |

**Luma Spaces — Phase 1 Priority (to be built):**
The current platform supports a basic event content type but lacks a dedicated Luma Spaces experience. Event discovery, registration, attendance tracking, and sponsor integration must be scoped and built as an early priority — this is the platform's signature differentiator.

---

### Out of Scope for MVP

| Feature | Reason |
|---|---|
| **Marketplace** | Excluded by design — not aligned with La Luma's community focus at launch |
| **Full event ticketing & payments** | Lightweight event listings in MVP; dedicated ticketing infrastructure is Phase 2 |
| **Brand partner self-serve portal** | Early partnerships managed directly; portal is a scale-phase feature |
| **Multi-language localisation** | English-first launch; localisation built into Phase 2 roadmap |

---

### MVP Success Criteria — When to Scale Beyond Launch

- 30%+ retention of first-cohort users after 30 days
- Consistent weekly return rate of 30–40% across active users
- At least 1 Luma Spaces event completed with 50+ attendees
- First brand partnership signed and activated
- Qualitative member feedback confirming the "I belong here" moment
- No significant community safety incidents under current moderation approach

---

### Future Vision — La Luma in 2–3 Years

- **Global reach:** Localised versions across multiple languages and regions, beginning with highest single-mother concentration markets
- **Luma Spaces at scale:** A recurring global event calendar with local chapters, sponsor integration, and hybrid online/offline formats
- **Brand marketplace:** A self-serve portal where aligned brands access the La Luma audience through verified, community-approved placements
- **Financial empowerment tools:** Partnerships with financial services, legal aid, and career platforms tailored to single mothers' economic realities
- **Mentorship layer:** Connecting experienced single mothers with those newly navigating solo parenting
- **Research & advocacy:** La Luma as a data and voice platform generating insights on single motherhood, informing policy and commercial decisions
